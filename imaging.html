Co-localization of DSSQ with TMRM in NIH 3T3 Cells with TMRM

NIH 3T3 cells were incubated for 30min in Hoechst, Tetramethylrhodamine (TMRM), and DSSQ. Images of the live cells were taken on a Confocal Microscope. A is Hoechst a nuclear dye. B is TMRM a mitochondrial dye. C is DSSQ which stains the entire cell green. D is composite image of all three dyes. Co-localization can be seen through the overlap of red and green colors. All images were taken under the same settings.


A.
Hoechst, a nuclear dye.
B.
TMRM, a mitochondrial dye.
C.
DSSQ, which stains the entire cell green.
D.
Composite image of all three dyes.

All images were taken under the same settings.
  • 30 min Incubation w/fluorescent dyes
  • Hoechst (Nucleus)
  • DSSQ (Experimental)
  • TMRM (Mitochondria) or LTR (Lysosome)

Co-localization of DSSQ with Lysotracker Red in NIH 3T3 Cells

NIH 3T3 cells were incubated for 30min in Hoechst, Lysotracker Red (LTR) ,and DSSQ. Images of the live cells were taken on a Confocal Microscope. A is Hoechst a nuclear dye. B is LTR a lysosomal dye. C is DSSQ which stains the entire cell green. D is composite image of all three dyes . No co-localization can be seen through the lack of overlap of red and green colors. All images were taken under the same settings.


A.
Hoechst, a nuclear dye.
B.
LTR, a lysosomal dye.
C.
DSSQ, which stains the entire cell green.
D.
Composite image of all three dyes.

All images were taken under the same settings.
  • 30 min Incubation w/fluorescent dyes
  • Hoechst (Nucleus)
  • DSSQ (Experimental)
  • TMRM (Mitochondria) or LTR (Lysosome)
